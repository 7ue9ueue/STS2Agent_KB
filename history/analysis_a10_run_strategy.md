# Ironclad A10 Run Strategy

Data from 40 successful Ascension 10 Ironclad runs. All numbers are observed in *winning* runs.

## HP Management

| Stat | Value |
|---|---|
| Average final HP % | 50.3% |
| Median final HP % | 53.4% |
| Runs ending >50% HP | 57.5% (23/40) |
| Runs ending 25–50% HP | 25.0% (10/40) |
| Runs ending <25% HP | 17.5% (7/40) |

- Finishing at 50% HP is *normal* for a winning run. Don't over-optimize for HP conservation.
- Average damage taken is almost identical across all three acts (~122–124 per act) — damage intake doesn't compress in Act 3.

## Campfire Strategy (**Critical correction to default GUIDE.md**)

Winning runs use campfires **2.5:1 in favor of upgrading over healing**:

| Option | Avg per Run | % of Uses |
|---|---|---|
| Smith (upgrade) | 6.2 | **71.7%** |
| Heal (rest) | 2.4 | **28.3%** |
| Other (Cook, Dig, Lift) | 0.5 | — |

- Average campfires per run: **8.9** (range 6–11)
- **Default choice = Smith.** Heal only when genuinely threatened — typically before bosses at low HP.
- The 2.5:1 ratio means even at 60% HP, top players often upgrade instead of healing.

## Elite Fighting

- **Average 6.8 elites per run** (min 3, max 10)
- Successful players fight elites very aggressively. More elites = more relics = better scaling.
- Damage taken per elite is low (13–23 avg), indicating elites are handled efficiently with Vul setups.

## Pathing Guidance (data-driven)

- **Elites as soon as viable.** At 6.8 per run with only ~20 damage per fight, the relic economy is worth the cost.
- **Campfire: default Smith, heal only before bosses or at <40% HP.** The 71% upgrade / 29% heal split is the correct ratio.
- **Boss card rewards: skip more than half the time.** Be very selective — 53% skip rate from top players.
- **Monster card rewards: skip ~31% of the time.** Don't add mediocre cards.
- **Shop visits: avg 2.85 per run (typically 2–3).** One early (Act 1/2 boundary), one mid (Act 2), possibly one late.
- **Final gold: median 74.** Players spend most of their gold. Don't hoard it.

## Damage Budget Per Act

Expect ~120–125 damage per act in a winning run. This is unavoidable — plan HP buffer accordingly:
- 17.5% of winning runs finish below 25% HP — razor-thin margins are winnable but not ideal
