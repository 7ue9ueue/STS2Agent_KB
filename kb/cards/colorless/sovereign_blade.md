## Card Text

## Notes

### Interactions

- Conqueror: Sovereign Blade deals double damage to the enemy this turn.
- Parry: Whenever you play Sovereign Blade, gain 6 Block.
- Summon Forth: Put Sovereign Blade into your Hand from anywhere.
    - Summon Forth will put all copies of the Sovereign Blade into your hand, including exhausted copies.
    - If the Sovereign Blade would not fit in the hand, it is instead discarded.
- Hammer Time: Whenever you Forge, all allies Forge as well.
- Seeking Edge: Sovereign Blade now deals damage to ALL enemies.
- Sword Sage: Sovereign Blade now hits an additional time.
    - If played before Void Form, Void Form will still make Sovereign Blade free. If played after Void Form, Sword Sage will apply after Void Form, so Sovereign Blade will now cost 1 energy. It is unclear if this is intended behavior or not (v0.99.1).

As a Colorless card, Sovereign Blade has some more specific interactions:
- Arsenal: Whenever you play a Colorless card, gain 1 Strength.
- Heirloom Hammer: Choose a Colorless card in your Hand. Add a copy of that card into your Hand.

=== Synergies ===
- Due to how quickly the damage value of Sovereign Blade can be raised, any multiplicative effects are highly effective at increasing its damage, including Vulnerable, Conqueror, and Flanking.
- Effects that reduce the Deck, such as Tyranny, or draw cards, such as Glow, will help cycle the Deck more quickly to redraw Sovereign Blade.
- Moving the Sovereign Blade around by means of Summon Forth or Cosmic Indifference will increase the frequency with which it can be played in a Deck cycle.
- If playing as the Necrobinder, and the player has attained Forge cards by means of the Prismatic Gem or Sea Glass, the Bookmark has a chance of lowering the -cost of Sovereign Blade.

=== Neutral ===
- Because the damage value Sovereign Blade can reach is so high, additive effects such as Strength and Vigor effects negligibly contribute to the damage of Sovereign Blade as it scales up.
    - If building a deck centered around Forging the Sovereign Blade, Multiplicative effects will have a greater effect than additive effects for increasing damage.
=== Anti-Synergy ===
- Sovereign Blade matches up poorly against enemy powers that reduce the amount of oncoming attacks, such as Slippery and Hard to Kill.
