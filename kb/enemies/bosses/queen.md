# Queen

## Overview

**Queen** is a Boss found in Glory. She fights alongside the Torch Head Amalgam.

## Stats

### Queen
- **HP**: 400 (Asc8+: 419)
- **Appears in**: Debut Glory

### Torch Head Amalgam

## Pattern

The Queen's pattern depends on whether the Torch Head Amalgam is alive:

**Opening (always):**
1. Puppet Strings
1. You're Mine

**While the Amalgam is alive:**
: Repeats Burn Bright for Me.

**After the Amalgam dies:**
: If the Queen is currently using Burn Bright for Me, she immediately switches to Enrage, then enters the enraged loop:
Keep in mind you will have vulnerable applied so the actual damage numbers are 7x5 and 25
1. Off with Your Head
1. Execution
1. Enrage
1. Back to Off with Your Head
## Torch Head Amalgam

### Pattern
The Torch Head Amalgam follows a fixed sequence:
1. Tackle
1. Tackle
1. Beam
1. Weak Tackle
1. Weak Tackle
1. Back to Beam (repeats steps 3-5)

## Notes

- After a Bound card is played, other Bound cards cannot be played that turn even with effects like Sly or a Duplicator.
- Transforming a Bound card during battle will remove Bound from it, allowing it to be played freely.
## Trivia

- The Queen is based on The Collector, an Act 2 boss in the original Slay the Spire.

## Strategy Notes

<!-- Reserved for the meta-agent. Append lessons, combos, and heuristics here.
     Wiki facts live above the divider. -->
- 161-win corpus: common Act 3 boss (107 fights), averaging 30.6 damage and 7.0 turns.
- Lower median damage than Test Subject/Doormaker; still punish slow decks without consistent block.
- After You're Mine, Torch Head Amalgam's Beam can show as 12x3 from player Vulnerable; kill Amalgam before this turn or hold reliable single-card block/potion coverage because Bound can prevent multi-card blocking.
- If the run can only gain one pre-Queen rest and has no potion held for floor 48, treat any optional Act 3 elite that risks dropping HP below a defended `You're Mine` Beam floor as a skip.
- Both target orders can work, but Queen-first asks the deck to break through repeated 20 Block from Burn Bright for Me; low-DPS decks should usually handle Torch Head Amalgam first.
- Vicious/Vulnerable burst, Strength-backed multi-hit, Fiend Fire, Whirlwind, Bully, Dismantle, or potion-backed big attacks are strong Amalgam-kill plans before Beam.
- Bring a one-card defense plan for the debuffed Beam turn: Impervious, Flame Barrier, Blood Wall with enough HP, Unmovable-boosted block, Buffer, Intangible, or a defensive potion.
- Barricade/Unmovable/Body Slam can beat the Weak damage penalty by converting block into damage, but it must be installed before Bound/debuff pressure takes over.
