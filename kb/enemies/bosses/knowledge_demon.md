# Knowledge Demon

## Overview

**Knowledge Demon** is a Boss found in Hive.

## Stats

### Knowledge Demon
- **HP**: 379 (Asc8+: 399)
- **Appears in**: Debut Hive

### Attacks

| Name | Intent | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Curse of Knowledge |  | Each player chooses one of two curse cards to receive. The options change each time this move is used. |
| Slap |  | Deals 17 damage. |
| _(Asc variant)_ | | Deals 18 (Asc9+) damage. |
| Knowledge Overwhelming |  | Deals 8 damage ×3. |
| _(Asc variant)_ | | Deals 9 (Asc9+) damage ×3. |
| Ponder |  | Deals 11 damage. Heals 30 HP per player. Gains 2 Strength. |
| _(Asc variant)_ | | Deals 13 (Asc9+) damage. Heals 30 HP per player. Gains 3 (Asc9+) Strength. |

## Pattern

Knowledge Demon repeats a fixed four-move cycle:
1. Curse of Knowledge
1. Slap
1. Knowledge Overwhelming
1. Ponder

After the third Curse of Knowledge (once all three curse sets have been offered), the cycle changes: Curse of Knowledge is skipped, and the remaining three moves repeat in order.
## Notes

- Curse of Knowledge curse sets:
    - Set 1: Disintegration (6 damage/turn) or Mind Rot (draw 1 fewer card/turn)
    - Set 2: Disintegration (7 damage/turn) or Sloth (max 3 cards/turn)
    - Set 3: Disintegration (8 damage/turn) or Waste Away (1 less Energy/turn)

## Strategy Notes

<!-- Reserved for the meta-agent. Append lessons, combos, and heuristics here.

- 161-win corpus: averages 30.4 damage and 6.7 turns over 62 fights.
- Fast fight by boss standards; prioritize tempo and clean damage windows.
- Taking repeated Disintegration preserves deck velocity but creates a hard HP clock; only choose it when the deck can race the remaining cycle.
- In draw-heavy Ironclad decks, Sloth can be worse than Disintegration because it blocks Bloodletting/Vicious/Battle Trance burst turns.
- Power Potion Crimson Mantle can stabilize the fixed attack cycle, but its 1 HP start-turn upkeep becomes lethal at very low HP; do not treat it as free block in long Knowledge Demon fights.
- At low HP, Mind Rot then Sloth can outperform Disintegration-first lines, but the deck still needs enough high-impact 3-card turns to kill before the repeat attack cycle.
