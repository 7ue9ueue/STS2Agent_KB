# Exoskeleton

## Overview

**Exoskeleton** is a normal Enemy found in Hive.

## Stats

### Exoskeleton
- **HP**: 24-28 (Asc8+: 25-29)
- **Appears in**: Debut Hive

### Attacks

| Name | Intent | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Skitter |  | Deals 1 damage ×3. |
| _(Asc variant)_ | | Deals 1 damage ×4 (Asc9+). |
| Mandibles |  | Deals 8 damage. |
| _(Asc variant)_ | | Deals 9 (Asc9+) damage. |
| Enrage |  | Gains 2 Strength. |

## Pattern

Exoskeletons appear in groups of 3 or 4. Each one's opening move depends on its position in the group: the first uses Skitter, the second uses Mandibles, the third uses Enrage, and the fourth (if present) randomly picks Skitter or Mandibles.

After Skitter, the next move is randomly Skitter or Mandibles (cannot repeat the same move twice in a row). After Mandibles, it always uses Enrage. After Enrage, the next move is randomly Skitter or Mandibles.
## In Party With

- 3 Exoskeletons (Weak)
- 4 Exoskeletons (Normal)

## Strategy Notes

<!-- Reserved for the meta-agent. Append lessons, combos, and heuristics here.
     Wiki facts live above the divider. -->
- Hard to Kill rewards many separate damage instances; Letter Opener, replay attacks, Anger copies, and Fiend Fire hits are more efficient than one large hit.
- Kill or heavily damage the Enrage-position Exoskeleton early so the group does not scale while capped damage slows kills.
- One-Two Punch plus Whirlwind can clear the group through repeated small AoE hits; wait/poll after Whirlwind because lethal replay damage may resolve after the immediate state response.
- Tear Asunder becomes efficient once HP-loss events build multiple hits; even modest 5-damage hits can finish low Exoskeletons through Hard to Kill.
- Flame Barrier is strong against Skitter because each reflected hit is a separate damage packet; Vambrace + Flame Barrier can cover the turn while dealing meaningful capped-safe damage.
