# Bygone Effigy

## Overview

**Bygone Effigy** is an Elite found in the Overgrowth.

## Stats

### Bygone Effigy
- **HP**: 127 (Asc8+: 132)
- **Appears in**: Debut Overgrowth

### Attacks

| Name | Intent | Effect |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Sleep |  | Does nothing. Asleep. |
| Wake |  | Gains 10 Strength. |
| Slashes |  | Deals 13 damage. |
| _(Asc variant)_ | | Deals 15 (Asc9+) damage. |

## Pattern

The Bygone Effigy starts asleep and follows this sequence:
1. Sleep — does nothing on its first turn.
1. Wake — wakes up and gains 10 Strength.
1. Slashes every turn after.
## Strategy

**Bygone Effigy** is a very dangerous Elite with a very simple weakness: hit it as hard as you can. You have two turns to attack it before it does anything; while it does gain 10 Strength on its second turn, this is just for flavor, as its 13(15) damage attack is only used after gaining Strength, making it functionally the same as a 23(25) attack. Your goal should be to deal as much damage as possible in those first three turns, in order to hopefully finish it off before it can attack.

Cards which do not deal damage are almost useless here, and your starter deck is generally not strong enough to win this battle on its own. Defend will only block 20% of the incoming damage from each attack from turn 3 onwards, and does nothing but increase Slow on the first two turns. Other defensive cards like Dodge and Roll or Shrug It Off are hardly any better, and adding them to your deck means you will draw attacks less often. If you plan to take on Elites in the Overgrowth, then your first priority should be to take as many strong attack cards as you can in order to be prepared in case you run into the Bygone Effigy. If you have not seen any strong attacks in early card rewards, it may be best to path away from elites until the second half of the Act rather than risk dying early.

If you cannot finish off the Bygone Effigy within three turns, then just keep hitting it as hard as you can and tank the damage. Don't play defensive cards unless you are absolutely certain you can finish it off on the next turn. Blocking 5 damage but then taking 23 damage because the Effigy survived an extra turn isn't worth it.

The Slow effect is usually not particularly impactful, but it does mean that extra card plays, such as from Shivs, will slightly increase the damage the Bygone Effigy takes. It is rare that you will have enough of a combo deck put together this early in the game to play large numbers of cards each turn.
