# Deck Size And Answer Density

Deck size is not a score. The question is whether the deck reliably finds its **specific answers** before scripted danger turns.

## Main Rule

- Once the deck has a working engine, protect answer density more aggressively than card quality.
- A card can be good and still be wrong if it delays Impervious, Apparition, Second Wind, Feel No Pain, Dark Embrace, or the deck's boss-kill package by one turn.
- In Act 3, medium-good cards are usually worse than skip, removal, potion support, or a decisive upgrade.

## Practical Size Gates

| Checkpoint | Warning size | Required justification |
|---|---:|---|
| End Act 1 | 22+ | Enough draw or frontload to beat the Act 1 boss |
| End Act 2 | 28+ | Engine complete, with draw/exhaust to shrink the live deck |
| Act 3 route | 32+ | Late picks must be premium, draw-positive, exhaust-positive, or boss-script answers |
| Forced Act 3 elite/boss | 35+ | Must have deterministic access to panic cards or a fast kill plan |

## Answer Density Check

Before taking a late card, pathing into an elite, or spending the final shop/campfire, name the answer cards for the next known threat:

- Heavy single hit: Impervious, Apparition, Weak, Second Wind, big retained/banked block.
- Status flood: Feel No Pain, Dark Embrace, Burning Pact, True Grit, Second Wind.
- Damage race: Vulnerable engine, Strength, burst potion, high-impact attack, repeatable draw/energy.
- Multi-enemy pressure: AoE, multi-hit, frontload plus block.

If the deck is large and the answer list is short, improve access before adding more output.

## Late Reward Rule

Take a late card only if it passes at least one:

- Draws or finds more cards than it costs in tempo.
- Exhausts junk or shrinks the live combat deck.
- Is a one-card answer to a known elite/boss turn.
- Directly upgrades the active engine's best turn.
- Solves a missing matchup that the current route cannot avoid.

Skip cards that merely add another attack, block card, or synergy piece without improving access to the cards that already win the run.

## Run 23 Lesson

Run 23 had many strong cards, but Mecha Knight exposed the answer-density problem. The deck contained Impervious, Apparitions, Second Wind+, Shrug It Off, Feel No Pain, and Dark Embrace+, but the lethal Heavy Cleave hand was Bloodletting / Juggling / Burn / Mad Science / Headbutt. The issue was not card quality; it was that a large deck plus Burns could miss the exact defensive answer on a scripted turn.

Reusable rule: once a run depends on specific answers, late decisions should prioritize skip, removal, draw, exhaust, upgrade, or potion over another medium-good card.
