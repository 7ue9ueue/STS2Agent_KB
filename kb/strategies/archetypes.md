# Ironclad Strategy Templates

These are templates, not forced plans. Start each run by solving the current act: damage in Act 1, a real engine and block in Act 2, refinement in Act 3. Pivot into a template only when the offered cards and relics support it.

Before adding late cards to any template, check [deck_size.md](deck_size.md). A complete engine loses if the deck is too large to find its answer cards on scripted danger turns.

## Template 1: Infinity / Fast Cycle

### What It Means

Infinity means the combat deck can repeatedly draw and fund itself. A full infinite is not required; a small deck that cycles Pommel Strike+, Shrug It Off, Bloodletting, and exhaust tools quickly is already a winning shape because it prevents bad draws and repeatedly finds the best cards.

### Core Pieces

| Role | Strong cards |
|---|---|
| Draw | Pommel Strike+, Shrug It Off, Battle Trance, Burning Pact+, Offering, Scrawl |
| Energy | Bloodletting, Offering, Pyre, Forgotten Ritual with exhaust support |
| Live-deck thinning | True Grit+, Burning Pact, Second Wind, Fiend Fire |
| Payoff | Vicious chains, Body Slam+, Bully/Dismantle/Whirlwind, any strong repeated attack |

### How To Draft

1. In Act 1, take Attack cards and frontloaded damage first. Favor attacks that also draw or enable tempo: Pommel Strike, Uppercut, Whirlwind, Dismantle, Bully, Anger, Twin Strike, and other real damage. Bloodletting and Battle Trance are excellent support, but they do not replace the need for enough attacks.
2. Remove Strikes/Defends when the deck already has replacement damage/block. In Act 1, keep removals balanced and synergy-aware: Perfected Strike/Hellraiser can make Strike density valuable, while Tighten-style effects can make Defend density valuable. In Acts 2/3, default to erasing Strikes first because starter Attack stats fall off, unless a live Strike payoff still justifies them. Do not remove so aggressively that Act 1 damage or block collapses.
3. Upgrade Pommel Strike early when possible. Pommel Strike+ is a key step from "good card" to "cycle engine."
4. Add exhaust selection after the first draw/energy shell appears. True Grit+ and Burning Pact turn statuses and starter filler into faster reshuffles.
5. By Act 2, stop adding medium cards that do not draw, make energy, exhaust junk, or solve a boss/elite check.

### How To Play Combats

1. Play draw cards before Battle Trance if their draw matters, because Battle Trance blocks further draw that turn.
2. Use Bloodletting when the extra energy converts into lethal, full block, key setup, or a phase skip. Do not spend HP for unused energy.
3. Exhaust Wounds/Burns/Dazed, starter filler, and spent setup cards. The goal is a smaller live deck for the next reshuffle.
4. Once the loop is small, count energy and draw before each play. The basic loop wants at least two draw cards and one energy-positive card.
5. Against Doormaker, be careful with pure infinite plans. Per-card taxes and draw denial can break a loop; prioritize efficient block, retained block, and Dark Embrace counterplay.

### When To Abandon

- The deck is too large and lacks enough draw/exhaust to shrink during combat.
- The run needs immediate Act 2 block or AoE more than another cycle piece.
- The boss punishes repeated card play more than the deck can exploit.

## Template 2: Body Slam / Defensive Setup

### What It Means

Body Slam+ converts defense into 0-cost damage. The plan becomes strongest when one card can create large block totals, or when Barricade lets block carry across turns.

### Core Pieces

| Role | Strong cards/relics |
|---|---|
| Payoff | Body Slam+ |
| Large block | Impervious, Blood Wall, Shrug It Off, True Grit+, Flame Barrier, Colossus, Taunt |
| Scaling block | Unmovable, Barricade, Feel No Pain, Corruption with enough skills |
| Support | Vulnerable, draw, energy, Anchor, Oddly Smooth Stone |

### How To Draft

1. Do not take Body Slam as a standalone damage card unless the deck already has real block or a clear path to it.
2. Upgrade Body Slam when the block package exists. The cost reduction is the point of the card.
3. Prioritize Unmovable highly when the deck has good block cards; Unmovable+ makes block turns much easier to execute.
4. Add Barricade when the deck can actually create surplus block. Barricade without large block can be too slow.
5. Keep drawing and energy cards. Body Slam decks still lose if they cannot find their block and payoff on the right turns.

### How To Play Combats

1. On attack turns, create block first, then play Body Slam after block is visible.
2. With Unmovable, make the first block card of the turn the largest or most important block card.
3. With Barricade, bank block before damage-race turns; Body Slam can then convert stored block into lethal.
4. Use Vulnerable before Body Slam when possible. A 0-cost Body Slam under Vulnerable is often the whole damage turn.
5. Against Frail, re-check block math. Unmovable doubles the actual block gained after modifiers, so Frail can still cause leaks.

### When To Abandon

- The deck has Body Slam but no large block, no Barricade, and no Unmovable.
- Act 2 elites/bosses require AoE or burst before a slow block engine can set up.
- The deck cannot draw Body Slam and block together reliably.

## Template 3: Vulnerable / Vicious Chain

### What It Means

Vicious turns Vulnerable application into draw. With Vicious+, each Vulnerable card can draw multiple cards, so Vulnerable becomes both damage scaling and card flow.

### Core Pieces

| Role | Strong cards |
|---|---|
| Engine | Vicious, Vicious+ |
| Vulnerable application | Bash+, Thunderclap, Tremble, Uppercut, Break, Shockwave, Vulnerable Potion |
| Payoff attacks | Bully, Dismantle, Whirlwind, Fiend Fire, Body Slam+, Dominate |
| Support | Pommel Strike+, Shrug It Off, Bloodletting, Battle Trance, Burning Pact |

### How To Draft

1. Take Vicious when the deck already has repeated Vulnerable or can reasonably find it soon. Upgrade it aggressively once the package is real.
2. Thunderclap is weak as a generic damage card, but it becomes a reasonable early speculative pickup when the deck wants AoE, needs Act 1 damage, and can later convert it with Vicious.
3. Keep Bash relevant with upgrades or transformation paths; upgraded Vulnerable duration matters.
4. Add payoffs after Vulnerable exists. Bully, Dismantle, Whirlwind, and Fiend Fire are much better when Vulnerable is reliable.
5. Do not take every Vulnerable card. The deck still needs block, draw, and energy.

### How To Play Combats

1. Play Vicious before applying Vulnerable unless immediate lethal requires a different order.
2. Apply Vulnerable before draw payoffs and major attacks.
3. If Vicious+ is active, treat each Vulnerable card as a draw card and re-read the hand after every play.
4. Against multi-enemy fights, Thunderclap can be both AoE setup and Vicious draw. It is much better when the following hand has AoE or multi-target payoff.
5. Save Vulnerable Potion for bosses/elites when it creates a phase skip or unlocks a Vicious draw burst.

### When To Abandon

- Vicious is offered but the deck has no Vulnerable cards and no time to wait.
- The deck has Vulnerable but lacks damage payoff, draw, or block.
- A boss or elite needs immediate defense/AoE more than another scaling card.

## Template 4: Dark Embrace / Exhaust Engine

### What It Means

Dark Embrace turns exhaust into draw. With Feel No Pain, the same exhaust also becomes block. This is a boss-scaling package because it shrinks the live deck, converts status cards into output, and can turn enemy exhaust taxes into an advantage.

### Core Pieces

| Role | Strong cards |
|---|---|
| Draw engine | Dark Embrace, Dark Embrace+ |
| Block engine | Feel No Pain, Feel No Pain+ |
| Controlled exhaust | True Grit+, Burning Pact, Second Wind, Fiend Fire, Corruption with enough skills |
| Repeatable fuel | Anger, status cards, generated cards, starter filler |
| Payoff | Body Slam+, Fiend Fire, Vicious/Vulnerable attacks, repeated 0-cost attacks |

### How To Draft

1. Take Dark Embrace more seriously once the deck already has controlled exhaust or an Act 3 boss that adds/exhausts cards.
2. Upgrade Dark Embrace when setup cost is the blocker; 1 energy is much easier to install before heavy boss turns.
3. Pair Dark Embrace with Feel No Pain whenever possible. Draw alone can still die to big attacks; draw plus block becomes a real engine.
4. Keep at least one controlled exhaust card upgraded. Random exhaust can delete the payoff; True Grit+ and Burning Pact let the engine choose fuel.
5. Anger improves sharply in this shell because it is 0-cost repeatable attack fuel instead of only an Act 1 bridge.

### How To Play Combats

1. Install Dark Embrace before spending major exhaust cards if the turn can survive the setup cost.
2. Exhaust statuses, starter cards, and already-used setup powers first; preserve single-copy block/damage payoffs until their job is done.
3. Against Doormaker Hunger, played Attacks and Skills exhaust. Dark Embrace can convert that tax into draw; Anger can become repeatable 0-cost damage if the live deck is thin enough.
4. Do not rely on this engine on Scrutiny turns because Doormaker blocks extra draw.
5. With Feel No Pain active, count exhaust events as block before deciding whether to spend HP-cost cards.

### When To Abandon

- The deck cannot afford a setup turn and has no opening block/potion.
- The deck has only random exhaust and too many irreplaceable one-copy answers.
- The boss turn blocks draw or taxes card plays in a way that prevents the engine from starting.

## Template 5: Boss Damage-Per-Round Scaling

### What It Means

Boss fights need a plan that improves output each cycle, not only a sequence of good standalone cards. The goal is to enter Act 3 bosses with at least one engine that makes turns 5-8 easier than turn 2.

### Core Packages

| Package | Scaling plan |
|---|---|
| Vulnerable/Vicious | Vicious+ turns Vulnerable cards into draw while Vulnerable multiplies every attack. |
| Strength/self-damage | Rupture+, Bloodletting, Blood Wall, Offering, Inferno, Inflame, Demon Form; pair with multi-hit or X attacks. |
| Block-to-damage | Unmovable, Barricade, large block, Body Slam+; defense becomes repeatable damage. |
| Exhaust value | Dark Embrace, Feel No Pain, True Grit+, Burning Pact, Second Wind, Fiend Fire; junk becomes draw/block. |
| Burst phase-skip | Vulnerable plus Bludgeon, Fiend Fire, Whirlwind, Bully, Dismantle, Body Slam+, or potion-backed attacks. |

### How To Draft

1. By mid Act 2, ask what the boss engine is. A deck with only decent standalone attacks and blocks can still fail Act 3 bosses.
2. Pick late cards by engine completion: take a narrow payoff if it completes the deck's scaling package, skip medium value that does not.
3. Preserve potions that complete a boss plan: Vulnerable, Strength, Intangible/Buffer, Gigantification, Liquid Memories, or a large defensive potion can be worth more than hallway efficiency.
4. For self-damage scaling, draft extra HP/block cushion. Rupture/Inferno lines are strong only if the deck survives the automatic HP tax.
5. For long fights, prefer scaling that also improves consistency: draw, exhaust, retained/banked block, or repeated Vulnerable.
6. In Act 3, once standard enemies are easy, card rewards must raise the ceiling or consistency of the boss engine. Skip medium cards that only make hallway fights easier.

### How To Play Combats

1. Spend early turns installing the engine only when the next attack is covered.
2. Use Headbutt and draw control to set known boss turns, especially Flame Barrier/Impervious for heavy attacks or Uppercut/Tremble before multi-hit turns.
3. Save burst for phase skips or scripted danger turns rather than using it on low-value early HP.
4. If the line reaches 1-4 HP with no potion and no imminent lethal, treat that as a failed setup plan and reload/adjust if possible.
