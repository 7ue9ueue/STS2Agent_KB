# Boss Strategy Summary

Canonical boss files live in [kb/enemies/bosses](../enemies/bosses).

## Start Of Act Checklist

- Read the visible boss file at the beginning of each act, not only before the boss.
- Let the boss change card picks, pathing, removals, shop buys, and potion priorities.
- Hold or replace potions for the expected hard turn rather than carrying generic value.

## Act 3 Boss Scaling Setup

- Do not enter Act 3 bosses with only standalone good cards. Have at least one scaling package: Vicious/Vulnerable, Dark Embrace/Feel No Pain exhaust, Unmovable/Barricade/Body Slam, Rupture/self-damage Strength, or potion-backed burst.
- Damage-per-round matters as much as current-turn survival. If a Doormaker or Test Subject line survives by exact block but boss HP remains high, the setup is failing.
- Preserve potions for a specific boss turn: Queen Beam after You're Mine, Doormaker post-Hunger Scrutiny/Grasp, or Test Subject Phase 2/3 burst.
- Headbutt is boss tech, not filler: use it to place Flame Barrier/Impervious before known heavy attacks, or Uppercut/Tremble before known multi-hit turns.
- Self-damage scaling needs an HP gate. Inferno, Blood Wall, Offering, and Bloodletting are strong only when the next two turns remain survivable after the HP tax.

### Doormaker Counterpackages

- Dark Embrace plus Anger can become a Hunger-turn loop once the live deck is thin enough: Anger is 0-cost, creates a copy, then Hunger exhausts the played Attack and Dark Embrace draws. Do not rely on this during Scrutiny because extra draw is blocked.
- Dark Embrace plus Feel No Pain turns Hunger's exhaust tax into draw plus block; this can make attacks/skills positive instead of depleting the deck.
- Barricade or Unmovable plus Body Slam+ is the clean defensive plan: bank block before Grasp, then convert block into damage.
- Vicious+ plus repeated Vulnerable gives the missing damage clock; pre-apply Weak/Vulnerable before Grasp when possible because Grasp makes reactive multi-card turns expensive.
- Avoid pure preservation lines. Doormaker's Strength gain eventually beats low-damage blocking even if the first cycle is clean.

### Queen Counterpackages

- Low-DPS decks should usually kill Torch Head Amalgam first; Queen-first must beat repeated 20 Block from Burn Bright for Me.
- Hold one-card or potion defense for the post-You're Mine Beam because Bound and 99 Frail/Vulnerable can make multi-card defense fail.
- Vicious/Vulnerable burst, Whirlwind/Bully/Dismantle/Fiend Fire, or potion-backed big attacks can kill Amalgam before Beam.
- Barricade/Unmovable/Body Slam can ignore much of the Weak damage penalty by turning defense into damage, but it still needs setup before debuffs lock the fight.
- If no potion is preserved and HP is low before Queen, route/rest decisions were probably the problem; do not count on exact Bound-turn blocking.

### Test Subject Counterpackages

- Phase 2 is the main deadline: repeated Multi-Claw gains hits every turn, so prepare either repeated block or a burst phase-skip.
- Save large burst for Phase 2 or non-Intangible Phase 3 turns. Phase 1 damage beyond the revive threshold is low value.
- Dark Embrace/Feel No Pain with controlled exhaust handles Burns and keeps the deck cycling across three phases.
- Demon Form, Rupture, Vicious, Barricade, and Unmovable are stronger here than in short fights because the fight gives time for scaling to pay back.
- Gigantification, Strength, or Vulnerable potions should be paired with a large single attack, Whirlwind, Fiend Fire, Body Slam+, Bully, or Dismantle to skip a dangerous phase.

## Status-Clog Bosses

- Bad cards from bosses are not only a penalty if the deck has controlled exhaust.
- Exhausting Wounds, Beckon, Frantic Escape after use, or other junk shrinks the live deck and improves future reshuffles.
